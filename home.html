Course Overview

Data Science is one of the fastest-growing fields of study in the world, with applications in domains ranging from advertising to zoology. Data drives billion-dollar decisions every day, but how exactly are those decisions made, and who benefits most from those decisions? In this course, you’ll get a taste of data science through an ethical framework. We’ll learn how to collect, process, and clean data; manipulate data in a variety of formats; contextualize variation and bias in data; visualize multidimensional data; and design accurate, clear and appropriate data graphics. Finally, we’ll apply these newfound skills to collect and analyze real-world data that’s important to YOU.

Expectations

You are not expected to have any prior programming experience for this class, but you are expected to be willing to try coding.

This course will include in-class discussions and activities, and lots of group work. Your teaching staff strives to create an equitable and inclusive environment so that you can bring your whole self to class, and can learn and grow as a data scientist and person. All students and teaching staff are expected to treat each other with fairness, kindness, and consideration.

Assignments

This course will include daily in-class activities, and homework. We encourage student collaboration, and ask that you record whomever you collaborated with on any assignments you complete. In-class activities may be completely as groups, but all group members must contribute code.

You will receive feedback on select assignments throughout the course, but no assignments will be graded.

Homework

Your nightly homework assignment is the following:

For each class period (morning and afternoon) record in your Progress Journal:

  1. Three things you learned in class (six total--3 for morning and 3 for afternoon)
  2. Choose one thing you learned and write a cheatsheet for it similar to the R cheatsheets we use (two total--1 for morning and 1 for afternoon) Be creative!
  3. At least one question you have about what you learned in class (two total--1 for morning and 1 for afternoon)
